Adventure of Yogi

Mountains are not fair or unfair, they are just dangerous. --Reinhold Messner

Nepal is a mountainous country where fourteen 8,000 meters high mountains of the world are located in this small landlocked himmalayan country. World highest peak of the world “Mt. Everserst” lies in this land. It is always the centre of attraction of the adventure lover and mountaineer.

Tough geographical location has made our lifestyle more tough. People from our community have a hard life and they choose hard things. Adventure is what they stream in their bloodline. This is how adventure and mountaineering has started in my life.

Mountaineering

Yogi started mountaineering in 2015 AD. In 2016, he was able to ascend Mt Amadablam which is at altitude of 6,856 m from sea level. This mountain is technically challenging and difficult to climb. The climbing grade is 5.7. Similarly, he successfully ascended the Mt. Lobuche, Mt. Island peak and Mt. Pokalde consequtively in a very short period of time.

The successful conquest of the 7,000 meters peak had motivated the Yogi to further the continuity of mountaineering. He successfully completed the training sessions conducted by the Nepal Mountaineering Association, Nepal Mountaineering Instructor Association, Nepal Mountaineering Guide Association and National Association of Tourism and Hospitality in various time periods.

Rock climbing and indoor climbing

Rock climbing is another adrenaline sport that Yogi loves when he is free. Climbing increases the physical endurance and agility of the human brain. It gives fitness and courage to start challenging work.

Yogi is licensed with the professional rock climber after completion of both the basic and advanced rock climbing course . He conducted many rock climbing camps for the students, professionals and military. He instructs and assists the beginners in indoor climbing as a professional instructor.

Kayaking and Outdoor activities

Rivers flowing from the snow capped peak have high current. Such rivers are good grade for kayaking and rafting. Nepal is abundant with rivers and rivulets. Tourists travel to Nepal for white water kayaking and rafting.

Yogi has the skill of kayaking and rafting. He does kayaking for fun and recreation. He loves to go kayaking on weekends. He also operated the rafting camps in the Bhote Koshi, Marshyangdi and Trishuli rivers. Bungee jump and Zipline are also another part of the recreation activities. Similarly, paragliding could be added in the packages and love to fly in the sky.
